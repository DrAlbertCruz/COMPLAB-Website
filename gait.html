Automatic Gait Abnormality Detection from Video

Non-invasive, Automatic Injury Detection with Consumer-Grade Camcorders

Personnel

  • PI: Alberto C. Cruz
  • Co-PI: Brian Street
  • Student Researchers: Andrew Hudson, Daniel Medina, Daniel Serrano, Eryn Chang, Guanrong Cai, Kristen Morgan, Matthew Duran, Natali Contreras, Nicholas Slinkard, Sydney Haynes

Abstract

Motion recognition systems have the potential to support clinicians, coaches and physical therapists in identifying important movement patterns which could aid injury detection potential or inform potential rehabilitation strategies. Current motion capture systems, wearables, structured light pattern and time-of-flight sensors have cost and utilization limitations that place constraints on their use outside of the laboratory setting. For this reason, we propose a system for motion recognition from consumer-grade video camcorders. The digital output from the recording will be processed by a project-developed algorithm to automatically identify movement linked to lower-limb pathology. Previous work by the proposal authors demonstrated the algorithm produced a 9.15% improvement in prediction accuracy over the other methods. This nascent method will be used to quantify the recovery of patients after total knee replacement surgery. The method presented in this work also has potential utility with other patient populations, such as Parkinson’s and Alzheimer’s disease, outside of laboratory setting to detect the degree of which, and progression of, gait pathology across these populations.



Preliminary Work



Example Front and Side Views of Patient

Examples of Motion Templates

The vision technology pioneered in this work summarizes a video in a way that prioritizes motion. Here we give an example video of a patient recovering from knee surgery, with identification marks removed to protect their identity, and a motion history image, an algorithm to summarize the temporal motion of the patient's gait. Motion templates offer insight into the movement pattern of a patient at a glance.
